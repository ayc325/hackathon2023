About The Problem

What is Bycatch?

Bycatch refers to the unintentional capture of non-targeted marine species in fishing nets and other equipment

What is Bycatch?

Bycatch is a significant issue for ocean conservation, as it leads to the death of millions of marine animals every year, and can also contribute to the decline of endangered species.

What Can We Do?

Changing fishing gear, modifying fishing practices, implementing fishing quotas, etc.

What Can We Do?

For example, using hooks and lines instead of fishing nets can reduce bycatch, as can using acoustic devices to deter marine mammals from fishing areas.

Why Is This Important?

Addressing bycatch is critical for the health of marine ecosystems and the fishing industry

Why Is This Important?

Reducing bycatch helps protect marine ecosystems and biodiversity while increasing the sustainability of fisheries and preventing the accidental capture of valuable species.
